Potential Energy

Potential energy is the amount of energy an object or system has at rest.

The more mass an object has, the more potential energy the object has. Potential energy cannot be tranfered to another object.

Types of Potential Energy

Chemical Energy
Chemical energy is energy stored by bonds. It can be released when bonds are broken.
Example: Substances like biomass, natural gas, petroleum, propane, cheesecake, and coal have large quatities of chemical energy.

Gravitaional Energy
This is energy of an object position. The high from the ground the object is, the more energy it has. Gravitaional energy can be calculated by multipling the altitude of an object and it's weight.
Example: If ball is placed at the top of a hill, it will have more energy than a ball at the bottom of the hill.

Nuclear Energy
Nuclear energy is the amount of energy stored by an atom's nucleus. This type of energy is released when the nucleus is split (fission) or joined with another atom's nucleus (fusion).
Example: Uranium stores a lot of nuclear energy.
