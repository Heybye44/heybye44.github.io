Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the amount of energy an object has in motion.

Kinetic energy can be tranferred in collisions.

Types of Kinetic Energy

Eletrical Energy
This is energy that defines the movement of electrons.
Example: Your phone, lamp, television, and computer all have electical energy.

Elecromagentic Energy
This type of energy is also called Radiant energy, and travels in the universe as tranverse waves. Radiant energy encompasses visible lights, x-rays, gamma rays, and radio waves.
Example: Sun light is a good example of eletromagnetic energy, along with x-rays that you get at the docter. Even Wi-Fi and 4G is made possible by eletromagnetic waves.

Sound Energy
When energy is transfer through objects via longitudinal waves, the objects have sound energy.
Example: The sound that you hear when a hammer hits a nail is sound energy traveling through the air and into your ears.

Mechanical Energy
This type of energy is sometimes considered kinetic or both, so it has been placed on the front page.
Example:: Your phone, lamp, television, and computer all have electical energy in their wires.
